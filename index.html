Military

Sworn in on March 27, 2012, I am serving in the United States Army Reserve’s as a 68A (Biomedical Equipment Technician). The definition of a Biomedical Equipment Technician (BMET) according to the Army is

“Performs maintenance series and repairs of all medical equipment. This equipment employs mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic, compressed gas and steam, electrical/electronic, solid state, digital, microprocessor, optical, and radiological principles.“

In short, we perform maintenance and repair on any medical equipment. The 68A course is one of the longest and toughest AIT courses in the 68 series, with the course being 42 weeks long and having a high drop out rate. The 68A course is located on Fort Sam Houston in San Antonio, Texas. It consists of 12 courses, each course dealing with a variety of different equipment. During each course, you had a lecture and lab portion. During the lecture portion you were expected to take up to 6 quizzes a day to test your knowledge of the subject. From the lecture to the lab you were expcted to troubleshoot and perform preventative maintenenace on the equipment for that specific course. Depending on the course, you could have as little as 2 peices of equipment to work on or up to 5 pieces of equipment. The 68A course is designed to teach you preventative maintenance and troubleshooting skills on many different equipment. As well as teaching you to look for the small pieces of information to better be able to service a piece of equipment.

Here I learned a great deal more about the garden department which included the different types of plants that Home Depot carried as well as all of the mulch and fertilizer. During my time of being on the garden recovery team, I was liscensed on how to drive the different machines that Home Depot used. I was given my license for the hi-lo fork lift, reach tuck, order picker, electric pallet jack, and slip sheet. To increase my skills as a worker at Home Depot, I would regulary take morning shifts working in the garden department and hardware department to learn more about the operation and management of the store. During my 5 month internship with Wineman, I learned the style of designing with LabView and also developed and maintained the virtual machines for the support of legacy code. Once my time was finished at Wineman I accepted at job at Fiat Chrysler Automotive (FCA).
